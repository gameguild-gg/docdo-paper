# S11: Paywalled Article Access Instructions

This document provides guidance for accessing paywalled articles included in this systematic review.

## 1. Institutional Access

Many articles are available through institutional library subscriptions. Check your institution's access to:

- **IEEE Xplore** (ieeexplore.ieee.org)
- **ScienceDirect** (sciencedirect.com)
- **Springer Link** (link.springer.com)
- **Wiley Online Library** (onlinelibrary.wiley.com)

## 2. Interlibrary Loan (ILL)

If your institution does not have access, request articles through Interlibrary Loan:

1. Contact your library's ILL service
2. Provide the DOI or full citation
3. Typical turnaround: 1-5 business days

## 3. Author Requests

Many authors provide preprints or author-accepted manuscripts upon request:

1. Find author contact via institutional website or Google Scholar
2. Send a polite email requesting a copy for research purposes
3. Reference the specific paper by DOI

## 4. Legal Preprint Sources

Check these sources for legal preprint/postprint versions:

- **PubMed Central** (ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc)
- **Europe PMC** (europepmc.org)
- **Author institutional repositories**
- **ResearchGate** (researchgate.net) - author-uploaded versions
- **Semantic Scholar** (semanticscholar.org) - open access links

## 6. Publisher-Specific Notes

### IEEE Publications
- Many IEEE papers have author-accepted manuscripts on arXiv
- IEEE authors may share via author preprint rights
- Check IEEE Xplore "All Media" tab for supplementary materials

### Elsevier Publications (Medical Image Analysis, etc.)
- 12-24 month embargo before open access
- Authors may share accepted manuscripts immediately
- Check ScienceDirect for "Share Link" option

### Springer Publications (MICCAI proceedings, etc.)
- SharedIt links allow free read-only access
- Conference papers often have arXiv preprints
- Check author ResearchGate profiles

### Nature Publications
- Nature Communications is fully open access
- Nature Methods has selected open access
- Check PMC for funded research

## 7. Fair Use Statement

This systematic review cites paywalled articles in accordance with academic fair use principles. Citations reference publicly available metadata (titles, abstracts, DOIs) and report findings for scholarly purposes. Direct quotations are minimal and properly attributed.

## 8. Data Extraction Verification

For verification of extracted data from paywalled sources:

1. **Table values**: Extracted directly from published tables
2. **Metrics**: Reported as stated in original papers
3. **Methods descriptions**: Summarized from published methods sections
4. **All sources**: Documented in S8_table_sources.csv with page numbers

Reviewers with institutional access can verify any extraction by consulting the original source at the cited page/table location.
